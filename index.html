About

I'm a space technology professional specializing in space instrumentation and mission design. Currently pursuing a Master of Space Studies (Track: Space Engineering and Applications) at the International Space University, Strasbourg France. I combine expertise in optical design, space systems engineering, and advanced data analytics. My background bridges astrophysics research and practical engineering—from analyzing Planck CMB data through the ESA Archival Research Visitor Programme to developing machine learning algorithms for cosmic signal extraction. I leverage Python, high-performance computing, and ML techniques to solve complex problems in space applications. I'm passionate about transforming scientific requirements into operational space technology. My focus areas include space/ground instrumentation design, optical systems, and computational methods for mission analysis. I seek opportunities to contribute to next-generation space missions where innovation meets exploration.
